Data Root Bridge

Data Root Bridge is a live interactive project that connects India's Living Root Bridges with microbial data. It shows how our small everyday choices affect the Earth's hidden ecosystem. As you change the parameters, the root bridge grows and changes in real time. This project makes invisible ecological networks visible, helping us shift from a human-centered view to a nature-centered way of thinking.

Visual Motivation

Jingkieng Jri: Living Root Bridge

The Living Root Bridge is a prime example of how indigenous communities share and use nature as a common resource based on traditional ecological knowledge. To survive in a heavy rain environment, people guided the aerial roots of rubber trees to entangle and take root across rivers. Over time, these roots formed strong, living bridges. This architecture represents not only structural strength but also long-term co-existence for future generations.

Design Concept

This platform connects real-world climate, atmospheric data, and global microbiome archives. It dynamically links historical changes in climate and soil with real-time pollution data from citizen sensors. Through human intervention parameters, users directly influence how the living root bridge grows and connects.

Used Data

* Earth Microbiome Project (EMP Active Archive)
Historical baselines for soil health and microbial diversity.

* Open-Meteo API
Real-world air temperature, soil temperature, and moisture.

* Sensor.Community
Live, crowd-sourced fine dust data from local citizen sensors.
